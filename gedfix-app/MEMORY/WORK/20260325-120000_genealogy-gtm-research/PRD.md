## Context

User is building GedFix, a commercial genealogy software product with AI features, targeting launch in 2026. The product has a KMP (Kotlin Multiplatform) desktop app with GEDCOM processing, AI-powered story generation, contradiction detection, DNA tools, and research logging. User needs comprehensive go-to-market intelligence covering market sizing, pricing, distribution, legal/business formation, and partnership opportunities. Special focus on Jewish genealogy and Eastern European records as underserved niches.

### Risks
- Market data may be outdated or conflicting across sources
- Conference/partnership details change annually
- API availability and pricing from Ancestry/MyHeritage may be under NDA
- GDPR specifics for genealogy data are complex and evolving

### Plan
Five parallel research streams executed via extensive web search: market sizing, competitor pricing, distribution channels, business formation, and partnership opportunities. Results synthesized into comprehensive report with verified sources.

## Decisions

- Recommended Paddle/Lemon Squeezy over Stripe for initial payment processing (MoR benefits)
- Recommended Open Core licensing strategy (open source GEDCOM library, proprietary AI/UI)
- Recommended FamilySearch API as first integration priority (free, well-documented, largest dataset)
