## 2. Geneteka (geneteka.genealodzy.pl)

Geneteka contains approximately 65.3 million records (33.9M births, 22.1M deaths, 9.2M marriages) across all indexed Polish locations. The database is free, no login required.

**Search instructions for Mallinger:**
1. Visit [geneteka.genealodzy.pl](https://geneteka.genealodzy.pl/index.php?op=se&lang=eng)
2. Enter "Mallinger" in the surname field
3. Search across all 16 voivodeships plus Ukraine/Belarus/Lithuania
4. Filter by Lodz province for localized results
5. Records coded: U = births, M = marriages, Z = deaths

**Important limitation:** Geneteka indexes are volunteer-created. Coverage varies significantly by region. Some areas have extensive Jewish record indexing; others have primarily Catholic parish records. Lodz Jewish records may have incomplete coverage.

**Archive reference:** Polish Genealogical Society (Polskie Towarzystwo Genealogiczne) database

## 4. 1897 Russian Empire Census

**Critical finding: Census returns for Lodz and most of Congress Poland were destroyed.**

The 1897 All-Empire Russian Census was the first and only census of the Russian Empire. Congress Poland contained 1,321,100 Jews (14.1% of population). However, after statistical data was gathered, most census returns were destroyed. Some original returns survive for Lomza and surrounding areas, but **no surviving 1897 census returns have been identified specifically for Lodz**.

**Alternative: Books of Residents (Ksiegi Ludnosci)**
- Lodz has surviving Books of Residents from **1827 onward**
- These are ongoing census-like records organized by house number
- Each record includes: name, parents' names, birth date/place, marital status, religion, social status, residence, occupation, and notes on moves/marriages/deaths/military
- Written in Polish (except 1868-1914 in Russian)
- Alphabetical index ledgers were created for each series (not all survived)
- Searchable through JRI-Poland and Polish State Archives

**JewishGen Belarus project** has extracted Jewish records from the 1897 census for Belarus territories, but Congress Poland records are largely lost.

**Archive references:**
- [JRI-Poland Books of Residents](https://jri-poland.org/bor.htm)
- [JewishGen 1897 Census Extracts](https://www.jewishgen.org/belarus/lists/intro_1897_russian_census.htm)
- Ancestry collection: [Russia, Jewish Families in Russian Empire Census, 1897](https://www.ancestry.com/search/collections/1448/)

## 6. Gesher Galicia and Regional Jewish Genealogy Projects

### Gesher Galicia - All Galicia Database
- Free search engine with 900,000+ records from former Austro-Hungarian Galicia (1786-1945)
- Covers eastern Poland and western Ukraine
- Record types: Jewish vital records, cadastral landowner lists, school/voter/tax/military records
- Search at [geshergalicia.org/all-galicia-database/](https://www.geshergalicia.org/all-galicia-database/)

**Important geographic note:** Lodz and Tomaszow Mazowiecki were in **Congress Poland (Russian partition)**, NOT in Galicia (Austrian partition). Gesher Galicia records would only be relevant if Mallinger family originated in or had connections to Galicia before moving to the Lodz region.

### Bukowsko, Galicia Connection (SIGNIFICANT FINDING)

A Geni.com genealogy project titled "Jewish Families from Bukowsko, Poland" lists **Blima Mallinger (nee Josefovitz), died 1961** as connected to the Bukowsko Jewish community.

**Bukowsko details:**
- Located in Sanok district, formerly in Austrian Galicia (Lwow province)
- Jewish Kehilla district: Bukowsko Miasto
- Jewish records from Bukowsko cover 1874-1939
- Civil records cover 1890-1914
- Marriage banns records 1850-1975
- In spring 1942, 804 Jews of Bukowsko and 300+ from surrounding villages were forced into a ghetto

## Surname Analysis

**Origin:** Mallinger is a habitational surname from Germanic traditions, referring to localities named "Malling" (notably in Bavaria). Derives from Old High German "malan" (to grind), associated with milling/mill settlements.

**Jewish adoption:** Adopted by Ashkenazi Jewish communities following late-18th century edicts (e.g., 1787 Austrian decree requiring hereditary surnames).

**Variant spellings:** Mellinger, Mellenger, Malenger, Malinger

**Global distribution (forebears.io):**
- United States: 749 people
- Austria: 438 people (strongest European concentration)
- France: 305 people
- Luxembourg: 95 people (highest density per capita)
- Germany: 79 people
- Romania: 65 people
- Israel: present but count not specified

**Notably absent from distribution:** Poland and Russia show no modern-day Mallinger population in the forebears.io data, consistent with Holocaust destruction of Polish Jewish communities and post-war emigration.

## Lodz Ghetto Database

The Lodz-Names database contains approximately **240,000 records** of Lodz Ghetto inhabitants (1940-1944). Searchable by surname with Daitch-Mokotoff phonetic matching.

Each record includes: surname, given name, maiden name, sex, date of birth, occupation, ghetto address, apartment number, and notes (deportation dates, transport numbers, death information).

**Search at:** [JewishGen Lodz Ghetto Database](https://www.jewishgen.org/databases/poland/lodzghetto.html)

**Note:** Volume Five of the printed Lodz-Names contains approximately 20,000 additional entries not in the online database. For comprehensive research, consult printed volumes at major research libraries.
