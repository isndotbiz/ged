## Context

Charles Mallinger's GEDCOM lists "Kalush" as a residence. This appears to be Kalush (Kalusz), a town in Austrian Galicia (now Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast, Ukraine). Previous research identified Jewish Mallingers in the Sanok/Bukowsko area of Galicia and suggested a Galician origin before the family moved to Congress Poland (Lodz/Tomaszow Mazowiecki). Kalush could be the missing geographic link in this migration chain.

### Risks
- Kalush vital records may not be indexed or accessible online
- The "Kalush" residence entry in the GEDCOM may be a transcription error or refer to a different place
- Connection between Kalush district and Sanok district is geographic speculation without record evidence
- Skrobisz/Scrobish may be a purely Polish (non-Galician) surname

## Decisions

- Kalush (Kalusz) is definitively in a DIFFERENT Galician district than Sanok -- they are ~150km apart
- Mallinger IS listed in Beider's Dictionary of Jewish Surnames from Galicia, confirming it as a known Galician Jewish surname
- Two Kalush landsmanshaftn existed in New York (YIVO RG 123), confirming emigration from Kalush to America
- Kalush vital records are held at TsDIAL (Lviv) Fond 701 Series 1, and DAIFO (Ivano-Frankivsk)

## Verification

All findings verified through web searches of JewishGen, Gesher Galicia, YIVO archives, Yizkor book project, and multiple secondary sources. Population data cross-referenced between Yizkor book and Jewish Galicia & Bukovina portal.
